Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

A login to the Electronic Records Submission Tool is granted upon approval of a Submission Agreement. The IASC staff will send you an email when it has been activated. A Submission Agreement can be completed here.

What kind of records can I send?

The IASC only accepts electronic records (computer files) of permanent retention. These are generally records of historical, administrative, or legal value and should be transferred to the Archives for preservation. More information can be found

What are permanent records?

Permanent records are those kept for their historical, administrative, or legal value to the university. They are designated as having "permanent" retention schedules. The university's retention schedules are available. If you have questions determining whether a record is permanent, contact the IASC Staff here.

My electronic records are on a CD/DVD (etc.). Do I use this tool to transfer to archives?

For permanent electronic records on media, follow procedures for paper transfer. Alternatively, you could transfer the files from the media using this tool. In this case, the archives would not need the media.

I have multiple folders and files to send. How do I transfer them?

If the folders aren't important, then you can just select the files as done for a regular transfer. In the situation where the folder structure (ie. hierarchy) is important, the transfer can be made in two ways. One method is to create a zip file. The zip file can then be transferred to us using ERST. The second method is to put the folders on external media such as a flash drive, external hard drive or optical media (CD or DVD). This media can then be dropped off at the archives in Conrad Hall. If you need media, please contact us at or email.

Do I need to create a Submission Agreement every time I want to send electronic records?

No, a Submission Agreement need only be created once per office.

Where do I enter an inventory?

The website automatically creates an inventory from the files selected for transfer. An email containing this list is sent to both the IASC and to the sender. The user can also create an inventory list and include as part of the file selection for transfer. Please label the file as "inventory."

What are access restrictions?

The records transferred should be free of any restrictions for viewing by university employees or the public. If some records need to be restricted from viewing, please note the restrictions on the transmittal form when the records are transferred. Restrictions could be due confidentiality information. If you have further questions, contact the University Archives staff.

Can I send records with a retention period?

The University only accepts electronic records of "permanent retention." For guidance on electronic records with a retention period, contact the IASC staff.

What if I need to add another user to send records?

Additional users can be added to an approved Submission Agreement for an office. Please contact the IASC staff.

I need to change the head of department (or other field) on my approved Submission Agreement. What do I do?

If you need to change information in your approved Submission Agreement, send an email with your Office/Department Name (registered in the Electronic Records Submission Tool), the field name that has new content and the new content.

I accidently sent an incomplete transfer request. I have more files to send. What should I do?

Send another transfer request with the missing files. Then either email the IASC to let them know, or you can also add a comment to this affect in the transfer request description/comments box explaining the situation. We'll link the two requests in our collection management system and put the downloaded files together.

How do I report a problem or bug or get more help?

If you are having a problem that can't be answered by the Help or FAQ or have found a possible software bug, please either call us at or email.
