The Department of Distinctive Collections (DDC) at MIT Libraries collects, preserves, and makes available digital material as well as paper and other analog formats.

This Digital Transfer Tool provides the ability for you to transfer digital material, including MIT Theses, from Institute offices to the DDC as the first step to including them in the Collections.

What does the Department of Distinctive Collections (DDC) collect?

In alignment with Institute policies and DDC collection development policies:

  • records that are generated or received by the various administrative and academic offices of the Institute in the conduct of their business,
  • teaching and research materials
  • correspondence with colleagues or professional organizations,
  • committee files
  • financial records (summary level, budgets) and annual reports
  • MIT Theses
  • Outreach and promotional material
  • and more...

Please note - if you are actively using the digital files, then they are not ready to be donated. Please contact us at ddc-offers (at) mit.edu with questions.

For more information on preparing and submitting materials, visit the Submission instructions.
